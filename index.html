Itinerary

Five days through the wine route, from Strasbourg to the southern villages. Pace is relaxed: long lunches, sunset views, and at least one dégustation per day.

Day 1 · Friday

17 July · Drive south & arrive in Colmar

  • 07:00 Depart from the Netherlands. Coffee, snacks, road-trip playlist on.
  • 10:30 Pit stop near Luxembourg / Metz — lunch break.
  • 15:00 Arrive in Colmar, our base. Check in, drop bags.
  • 16:30 Stroll La Petite Venise, get oriented, espresso on a square.
  • 19:30 Welcome dinner — Alsatian classic tarte flambée & a bottle of Riesling.

🚗 ~7 hrs driving (≈ 650 km). Split the wheel between Juup and one co-pilot.

Day 2 · Saturday

18 July · The Wine Route — northern villages

  • 09:30 Drive to Riquewihr — fairytale village, walk the ramparts.
  • 11:00 First tasting at a Grand Cru domaine (Schoenenbourg).
  • 13:00 Lunch in Hunawihr — local baeckeoffe or choucroute.
  • 15:00 Visit Ribeauvillé; optional splurge tasting at Trimbach 💎 (not cheap, but iconic Riesling).
  • 17:30 Detour to Château du Haut-Kœnigsbourg for sunset views over the plain.
  • 20:30 Dinner back in Colmar — winstub atmosphere.

🍷 Aim for max 2 tastings; spit, hydrate, designate a driver.

Day 3 · Sunday

19 July · Strasbourg — culture & cathedral

  • 09:00 Drive up to Strasbourg (~45 min).
  • 10:30 Climb Cathédrale Notre-Dame; catch the astronomical clock at 12:30.
  • 13:00 Long lunch in La Petite France.
  • 15:00 Pick one: Musée d'Art Moderne or Palais Rohan.
  • 17:00 Boat tour on the Ill (Batorama) — easy way to see the European Quarter.
  • 20:00 Apéro on Place Kléber, dinner in the old town.

🏛️ Sunday: confirm museum opening hours in advance.

Day 4 · Monday

20 July · Work morning, play afternoon

  • 08:30 Coffee & co-working from the gîte / a Colmar café with wifi.
  • 09:00 – 13:00 💻 Focused work block — laptops out, async standup, ship some code.
  • 13:30 Quick lunch — flammekueche to-go.
  • 14:30 Drive south to Eguisheim (most beautiful village in France).
  • 15:30 Detour to Gueberschwihr for a tasting at Domaine Ernest Burn 🍯 — the legendary dessert wines (per friend recommendation).
  • 17:00 Back to Eguisheim — walk the concentric streets, find the storks' nests.
  • 20:30 Dinner with a view of the vineyards.

🛜 Pre-check wifi at the accommodation. Backup: 4G hotspot from phones.

Day 5 · Tuesday

21 July · Last sips & drive home

  • 08:00 Breakfast, pack the car, load the wine boxes carefully. 📦
  • 09:30 One final stop in Kaysersberg for a coffee and a last walk.
  • 11:00 Hit the road north.
  • 14:00 Lunch break around Metz / Luxembourg.
  • 19:00 Home in the Netherlands. 🏠

🧊 Bring a cooler bag — summer heat is not friendly to white wine in the trunk.

Tips from the field

Crowdsourced wisdom from friends who have been before. Take seriously, but verify with our own palates.

“Trimbach — maar is niet goedkoop.”

— a friend who knows
Famous Ribeauvillé house, especially their Riesling Cuvée Frédéric Émile and the legendary Clos Ste Hune. Worth a stop, ideally with a wallet warmed up. 💳

“Vind Elzas heel lastig met wijn — Eric en ik zijn er in 2015 geweest, veelal niet onze smaak.”

— same friend, being honest
Fair warning: Alsace whites are aromatic and often off-dry, which isn't for everyone. Plan for variety — try Pinot Blanc, Sylvaner and Pinot Noir alongside the usual Riesling/Gewürz suspects.

“Wat wel heel bijzonder was, was Ernest Burn — waanzinnige dessertwijn, betaalbaar en erg goed.”

— the verdict
🍯 Domaine Ernest Burn in Gueberschwihr (15 min south of Colmar). Their Clos Saint-Imer Vendanges Tardives and SGN Gewürztraminer are the headline. Adding a tasting here to the schedule.

More from the internet hive-mind

Highly-rated picks from travel guides & reviews — beyond just wine.

🏛️ Culture

Unterlinden Museum, Colmar

Home of the Issenheim Altarpiece by Grünewald — consistently called one of the most powerful paintings in Europe. A 1-hour visit pays back tenfold.

🏛️ Culture

Strasbourg Cathedral

The 142m sandstone spire was the world's tallest building for 227 years. Climb the 332 steps to the platform and time your visit for the astronomical clock at 12:30.

🏛️ Culture

Château du Haut-Kœnigsbourg

Restored medieval fortress 700m above the Alsace plain. Reviewers love it at golden hour: panoramic views over vineyards stretching to the Black Forest.

🏛️ Culture

Écomusée d'Alsace, Ungersheim

Open-air museum with 70+ rebuilt traditional Alsatian houses. Quirky, charming, very family-friendly — top-tier on TripAdvisor.

🌲 Nature

Vosges Mountains & Route des Crêtes

Scenic ridge road through the Ballons des Vosges natural park. Sweeping views, high-altitude lakes, and grazing cows on summer pastures.

🌲 Nature

Lac Blanc & Lac Noir

Two glacial lakes in the Vosges, easy 2–3 hour loop hike. Perfect for a morning before an afternoon tasting.

🌲 Nature

Hohlandsbourg Castle ridge walk

Short hike just above Wintzenheim with three castles in a row (Pflixbourg, Hohlandsbourg, Hohnack) — castle nerds rejoice.

🌲 Nature

NaturOparC, Hunawihr

Stork & otter conservation centre. Ridiculously cute, especially with chicks in summer. Storks are the unofficial mascot of Alsace. 🦩

🏘️ Villages

Eguisheim

Voted “village préféré des Français.” Concentric streets, half-timbered houses dripping with geraniums. Less crowded than Riquewihr.

🏘️ Villages

Kaysersberg

Albert Schweitzer's hometown. Castle ruin with a view, a pretty fortified bridge, and a famously good Christmas market (not relevant in July, but still).

🏘️ Villages

Obernai

Less touristy gem north of Colmar. Sainte-Odile basilica nearby is pilgrim-busy but the views over the plain are magnificent.

🥨 Food

Try a real flammekueche

Thin-crust Alsatian “tarte flambée” with crème fraîche, onion, lardons. Reviewers swear by Au Brasseur in Strasbourg or any local winstub.

🥨 Food

Munster cheese

Pungent, washed-rind, made in the Vosges valley of the same name. Pair with a Gewürztraminer — locals insist this is the only correct combination.

🥨 Food

Kougelhopf for breakfast

Fluted brioche with raisins and almonds. Buy one fresh from a village boulangerie before noon — a beloved Alsatian ritual.

🍷 Wine

Domaine Weinbach, Kaysersberg

Biodynamic family estate run by the Faller women. Elegant, pure Rieslings — frequently top-rated. Booking essential.

🍷 Wine

Domaine Zind-Humbrecht, Turckheim

Olivier Humbrecht MW's biodynamic icon. Powerful, mineral, age-worthy — a benchmark for Alsace.

🍷 Wine

Crémant d'Alsace

Don't sleep on the bubbles. Excellent value sparkling — Pinot Blanc / Auxerrois / Riesling base. Try at Lucien Albrecht in Orschwihr.

Logistics

🚗 Travel

By car from the Netherlands. ~650 km / ~7 hrs each way via Luxembourg & Metz. Tolls in France — keep some euros & a card handy.

🏡 Base

Colmar — central on the Wine Route, easy day trips to Strasbourg, Eguisheim, Riquewihr.

💻 Work setup

Monday morning is a work block. Pick a place with reliable wifi; bring chargers, headphones, and a hotspot as backup.

🍷 Wine plan

Max two tastings per day. Spit at the first, sip at the second. Box per person to bring home — ~12 bottles fits comfortably.

🎟️ Book ahead

Cathedral platform tickets, Batorama boat, and at least one domaine tasting (esp. Saturday).

🌡️ Weather

July in Alsace: warm days (25–30°C), cool evenings. Light layers, comfy shoes, sunscreen.
